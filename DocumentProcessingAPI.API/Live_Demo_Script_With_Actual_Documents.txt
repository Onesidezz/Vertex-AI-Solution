SCENARIO 8: NO MODEL HALLUCINATION - GROUNDED RESPONSES
========================================================

**CRITICAL FEATURE: This application ONLY provides answers based on data we feed into it.**

**There is NO concept of Model Hallucination here.**

Let me demonstrate this important security and reliability feature:

**Example 1: Question Outside Our Data Scope**

Query: "Who is the CEO of OpenText?"

[Type and search - BEFORE processing the Announcement.pdf]

[Point to the response]

Response: "I am sorry, but the provided records do not contain information about the CEO of OpenText. The records are training materials for Content Manager and focus on product usage."

**This is exactly what we want!** The AI is being honest - it doesn't have that information yet, so it doesn't make up an answer.

**Now, let's process one document that contains our CEO information:**

[Demonstrate processing/syncing the Announcement.pdf document]

Now the system has ingested data related to CEO information. Let's ask again:

Query: "Who is the CEO of OpenText?"

[Type and search - AFTER processing the Announcement.pdf]

Response: "Ayman Antoun has been appointed as OpenText's CEO, effective April 20, 2026. He is a highly accomplished executive with more than three decades of global technology leadership experience, most recently serving as President of IBM Americas."

**Key Point:** If we feed or ingest data related to a topic, the system will definitely give accurate answers. We can ingest:
- HR Policies
- Company Policies
- Financial Reports
- Technical Documentation
- Any enterprise content

**Handling Contradictory Information:**

If we have policies from different years that contradict each other, the AI will be smart about it:

Example Response: "As per the FY 2025 policies, the work-from-home policy allowed 3 days per week. However, in the latest 2026 policy documents, this has changed to a phased return-to-office approach: 4 days in-office starting March 2026, moving to 5 days by September 2026."

The AI will cite the specific year and version, showing both policies and clarifying the timeline.


SCENARIO 9: CONTEXT UNDERSTANDING - NOT JUST KEYWORD MATCHING
==============================================================

**This application is NOT just matching keywords - it understands the CONTEXT and USER INTENT behind the question.**

Let me demonstrate:

**Example 1: Understanding Intent**

Query: "Find people who can help with content management implementation"

The system understands:
- "help with" = has skills/experience in
- "content management" = Documentum, OpenText, ECM systems
- "implementation" = project experience, not just theoretical knowledge

Results: Consultants with actual implementation experience in D2, InfoArchive, Content Manager

**Example 2: Semantic Relationships**

Query: "Who worked with pharmaceutical companies?"

The AI understands:
- "pharmaceutical" = "Life Sciences" = "Healthcare" = "Biotech"
- Finds: Ajith S who worked for Merck Germany (Life Science Customer)
- Even though the resume says "Life Science" not "pharmaceutical"

**Example 3: Understanding Numbers and Comparisons**

Query: "Invoices over five thousand dollars"

The AI understands:
- "five thousand dollars" = $5,000 = 5000
- "over" = greater than (>)
- Finds invoice with $6,965.91 total

**THIS APPLICATION WILL NOT GO OUT OF CONTEXT**

The system is designed to:
✓ Stay focused on the documents it has indexed
✓ Not make up information it doesn't have
✓ Ask for clarification if the query is ambiguous
✓ Cite sources for every answer
✓ Refuse to answer questions outside its knowledge base

If you ask "What's the weather today?" it will respond:
"I am sorry, but the provided records do not contain weather information. I can only answer questions based on the documents in Content Manager that have been processed."
